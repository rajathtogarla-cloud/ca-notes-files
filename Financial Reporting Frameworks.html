Core Logic

A General Purpose framework is designed for fair presentation and cannot be a compliance framework.

A Special Purpose framework can be either a fair presentation framework or a compliance framework.

General Purpose Frameworks

Financial statements prepared in accordance with a general purpose framework are designed to meet the common financial information needs of a wide range of users (e.g., investors, creditors, employees, public).

Why Fair Presentation?

Because the audience is broad and diverse, strict compliance alone might mislead some users. The framework requires the auditor to acknowledge that fair presentation is the goal, often requiring disclosures beyond the strict letter of the standard to achieve a "true and fair view."

Special Purpose Frameworks

Designed to meet the financial information needs of specific users. For example, financial statements prepared for a tax authority, a bank for a loan agreement, or a regulator.

As Fair Presentation:

If designed to give a true and fair view to that specific user group (e.g., IFRS for SMEs).

As Compliance:

If the user (e.g., a tax authority) requires strict adherence to a specific set of rules without regard for broader "fairness" concepts.

Key Distinctions: Fair Presentation vs. Compliance

Feature Fair Presentation Framework Compliance Framework
Primary Goal To achieve a "true and fair view" or "present fairly" in all material respects. Strict adherence to the requirements of the framework.
Management's Discretion Management may need to provide disclosures beyond specific requirements or, in rare cases, depart from a requirement to ensure fair presentation. Management strictly follows the rules; no departure or extra disclosure for "fairness" is typically anticipated by the framework.
