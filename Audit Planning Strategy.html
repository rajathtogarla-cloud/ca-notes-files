Strategic Imperative (SA 300)

The Partner's Mandate

Planning is not a discrete check-the-box exercise. It is a continuous, iterative process.

  • Scope & Boundaries: Explicitly identifies engagement characteristics to focus resources.
  • Reporting Objectives: Dictates timing of phases and nature of communication.
  • Directs Strategic Effort: Focuses team on high-risk areas.

Failure Case Study

XWL Limited

Commodity futures trading firm. Auditor CA P conducted the entire audit via email without visiting. Working papers used generic checklists for "raw material consumption"â€”a process non-existent in trading.

Result: Blatant violation of SA 300

The "So What?" Layer

Generic planning leads to a "shallow audit". If you don't understand the business, you cannot assess ROMM, missing risks of financial collapse.

Dilemma of Lowered Materiality

Lower materiality = Higher Audit Risk.

Nature, Timing & Extent (NTE)

Technical Directive on Samples

Precedent: CA Pradyuman

Sample sizes must be determined during planning, not execution.

  • Audit plan must explicitly describe extent.
  • Precision separates strategic audits from reactive ones.

Risk & Unpredictability

  • Unpredictability: Test assertions previously ignored.
  • Precise Timing: Distinguish interim vs. year-end.

Component (SA 600)

Cases: BBI Bank, Naveen Ltd

  • Principal auditor must state division of responsibility in report.
  • Not responsible for component work unless suspicion aroused.
  • Consider findings but no need to audit their papers.

Experts (SA 620)

Cases: Datacom Ltd, Black Mountain

Formal agreements required when matters are complex or data is sensitive.

Warning (KRP Ltd): Gross Negligence if you fail to verify expert's data. Reliance is not a shield.
