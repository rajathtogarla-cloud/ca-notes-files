Signed into law in 2002, the Sarbanes-Oxley Act revamped federal regulations related to corporate governance and reporting obligations to reduce corporate fraud.

Reduce Fraud

Tighten Procedures

SOX Section 404

The cornerstone of the act. It requires publicly traded companies to establish internal controls and procedures for Financial Reporting (FR).

Key Mandate

Companies must document, test, and maintain these controls to ensure effectiveness.

The Standard

Most companies and CPA firms use the:

COSO Internal Control — Integrated Framework

Management's Duty

Must assess if the system ensures reasonable assurance that material errors in Financial Statements will be prevented or detected.

SEC & PCAOB Requirements

Management

  • Assess Internal Control over Financial Reporting (ICOFR).
  • Test the design and effectiveness of controls.
  • Report effectiveness in the annual report.

External Auditors

Must provide 2 distinct opinions:

1. ICS Opinion Independent opinion on effectiveness of Internal Control System.
2. Financial Opinion Traditional opinion on the financial statements.
Mandatory

Internal Control Report Content

The SEC rules require the company’s annual report to include:

A.

Statement of Management's responsibility for establishing and maintaining adequate ICOFR.

B.

The specific framework used to evaluate effectiveness (e.g., COSO).

C.

Assessment of effectiveness, explicitly disclosing any material weaknesses.

D.

Statement that the public accounting firm has issued an attestation report.

Attestation Report Requirement

Companies must physically include the report from the registered public accounting firm that audited the Financial Statements.

"To protect investors by improving the accuracy and reliability of corporate disclosures."
