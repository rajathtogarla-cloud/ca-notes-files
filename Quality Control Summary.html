Quality Control in Audit Engagements

Standards and Implementation (SQC 1 & SA 220)

Integrity Review Compliance

Executive Summary

The integrity of financial reporting relies on a robust quality control system within audit firms. Governed by SQC 1 and SA 220, firms must prioritize quality over commercial gain.

Critical Takeaways

  • Priority of Quality: Overrides commercial strategy.
  • Client Vigilance: Rigorous evaluation of integrity & criminal history.
  • Listed Entities: Mandatory Engagement Quality Control Review (EQCR).
  • Rotation: Mandatory partner rotation (typically every 7 years) to mitigate familiarity threats.
  • Documentation: Files finalized within 60 days of report date.
"Quality must override commercial considerations."

I. Tone at the Top

Overriding Commercial Interests

Leadership fails when:

  • Commercials (fees) override quality.
  • Insufficient resources for quality policy development.

Personnel Sensitization

Offering "clean reports" for work or soliciting services via audit violates the Code of Ethics.

II. Client Acceptance

Evaluating Integrity

Reputation Control Attitude Criminal Activity Past Transitions

Check for money laundering, tax searches, or reasons for previous auditor resignation.

Withdrawal Procedures

  1. Discuss with Management & TCWG.
  2. Communicate reasons.
  3. Check reporting requirements to authorities.
  4. Document everything.

III. Ethics & Independence

Familiarity Threats

Long-term relationships with listed clients create familiarity threats.

The 7-Year Rule: Engagement partners for listed entities must rotate normally every 7 years.

Engagement Partner Role

  • Evaluate independence threats.
  • Assess breaches of policy.
  • Independence assessment must be reviewed by EQCR.

IV. Performance & EQCR

Engagement Quality Control Review (EQCR)

Mandatory for Listed Entities

Reviewer evaluates independence, judgments, and objectivity. They do not make decisions for the team.

Conflict Resolution

Consult other practitioners or regulatory bodies. Report cannot be issued until resolved.

Report Date Rule: Auditor's report cannot be dated prior to EQCR completion.

VI. Statutory Reporting (CARO 2020)

Specific vs. General Reporting

Compliance with frameworks like CARO 2020 is required under SA 220. "General" reporting (e.g., saying a company is "generally regular" without evidence) is unacceptable and constitutes a lapse in audit quality.
