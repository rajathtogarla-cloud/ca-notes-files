What is COSO?

The COSO Integrated Framework provides comprehensive guidance on establishing better internal controls to help companies achieve their objectives. It is the gold standard for internal control systems (ICS).

The 5 Components & 17 Principles

These 17 principles must be present and functioning in every entity for an effective Internal Control System.

1. Control Environment

Foundation of the system

  • 1 Demonstrates commitment to integrity and ethical values.
  • 2 Exercises oversight responsibility.
  • 3 Establishes structure, authority, and responsibility.
  • 4 Demonstrates commitment to competence.
  • 5 Enforces accountability.

2. Risk Assessment

Identifying what could go wrong

  • 6 Specifies suitable objectives.
  • 7 Identifies and analyzes risks.
  • 8 Assesses fraud risks.
  • 9 Identifies and analyzes significant changes.

3. Control Activities

Actions to mitigate risks

  • 10 Selects and develops control activities.
  • 11 Selects and develops general controls over technology.
  • 12 Deploys through policies and procedures.

4. Info & Communication

Flow of information

  • 13 Uses relevant, quality information.
  • 14 Communicates internally.
  • 15 Communicates externally.

5. Monitoring Activities

Assessing performance over time

  • 16 Conducts ongoing and/or separate evaluations.
  • 17 Evaluates and communicates deficiencies.

Total Integration

These components work together to form an integrated system that reacts dynamically to changing conditions.

Objectives of Framework

Operations

Focuses on efficiency and effectiveness of business operations, including performance and profitability goals and safeguarding assets.

Reporting

Pertains to internal and external financial and non-financial reporting. Ensures reliability, timeliness, and transparency.

Compliance

Adhering to laws and regulations to which the entity is subject, ensuring legal conformity.

Limitations

No matter how well-designed, internal control can only provide reasonable assurance to management and the board.

Not Absolute Assurance

Human Error

Mistakes in judgment or simple errors due to fatigue or distraction.

Management Override

High-level management may overrule prescribed policies.

Collusion

Two or more people conspiring to circumvent controls.

External Events

Events beyond the organization's control.
