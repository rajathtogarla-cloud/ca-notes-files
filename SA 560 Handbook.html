SA 560 Subsequent Events

Events between the financial statement date and the auditor's report date, and facts that become known to the auditor after the date of the auditor's report.

Objectives

  • Obtain evidence on events requiring adjustment/disclosure between FS date and Audit Report date.
  • Respond to facts becoming known after the Audit Report date that might change the report.

Audit Procedures to Identify Subsequent Events

Auditor must perform procedures for the period between FS Date and Report Date.

Inquiring Management on:

  • New commitments/borrowings
  • Asset sales or acquisitions
  • Appropriated/Destroyed assets
  • Contingencies developments
  • Unusual accounting adjustments
  • Going concern challenges

CASE I: The Gap Period

After Report, Before Issue

Generally, no obligation to perform procedures. However, if a fact becomes known that would have affected the report:

Immediate Actions:

  1. Discuss with Management/TCWG.
  2. Determine if FS needs amendment.
  3. Inquire how management will address it.

Mgmt Amends

  • Audit the amendment.
  • Obtain evidence.
  • Issue new report.

Mgmt Refuses

  • Not issued yet? Modify opinion (SA 705).
  • Already issued? Notify mgmt to stop distribution.
  • Still issued? Prevent reliance on report.

CASE II: Post-Issuance

After FS Issued

No obligation to check. But if a fact appears that would have changed the report:

Actions:

Discuss Determine Amendment Inquire Strategy

Option A: Mgmt Amends

  • Audit the amendment.
  • Review notification to previous recipients.
  • New Auditor's Report with Emphasis of Matter paragraph.

Option B: Mgmt Fails to Act

Auditor MUST take action to prevent reliance on the previously issued report and financial statements.
