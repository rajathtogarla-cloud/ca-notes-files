Framework Structure

The 20 criteria are grouped into 4 distinct, interacting areas.

1. Purpose

DIRECTION

Understanding the organization's direction, risks, and policies. It sets the stage for what needs to be achieved.

2. Commitment

IDENTITY & VALUES

The shared ethical values, integrity, and human resource policies that align identity with purpose.

3. Capability

COMPETENCE

Possessing the knowledge, skills, tools, and information required to execute the task effectively.

4. Monitoring

LEARNING & EVOLUTION

Reviewing internal and external environments to learn, adapt, and evolve the organization over time.

Scope of Control

Control is not just a top-down mandate. While it involves the entire organization, COCO emphasizes that it starts at the individual level.

Every employee plays a role.

Assessing Effectiveness

Effectiveness isn't a guess. To assess whether controls are working:

  • Identify specific criteria within the 4 areas.
  • Examine controls currently in place for each.
  • Verify they address the specific requirement.
