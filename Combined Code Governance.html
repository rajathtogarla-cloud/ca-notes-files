The Turnbull Report

Published by the ICAEW (Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales), this report provides essential guidance for listed companies on how to implement the Combined Code requirements regarding internal control.

Key Principles

Sound System

The board maintains a robust system of internal control.

Goal

Protect shareholders' investment and safeguard company assets.

Annual Review

Directors must annually review the effectiveness of the system.

  • Financial Controls
  • Operational Controls
  • Compliance Controls
  • Risk Management
Must report to shareholders

Internal Audit

Companies without an internal audit function are not exempt.

They must periodically review the need for establishing such a function.

Director’s Judgment

It is not a mechanical process. Directors must exercise professional judgment when reviewing how the company has implemented internal control requirements. Crucially, they must report these findings transparently to shareholders.

Risk-Based Approach

The board should adopt a risk-based approach to establish and review the effectiveness of the Internal Control System (ICS).

Integrated into governance processes

Integration into Management

"Internal control should not be a separate, one-time exercise for regulatory compliance but part of the company’s ongoing operations."
